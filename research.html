    High Minimum Wages in Good Times and Bad

    (with M. Reich and J. Wiltshire)
    View Abstract

    We present the first causal estimates of $15 minimum wage effects, focusing on fast food workers in 51 large U.S. cities and counties that reached $15 by 2022q1. Both stacked synthetic control and difference-in-differences estimators find substantial pay increases and non-significant disemployment effects. In good times, wage growth in low-wage states erodes some minimum wage pay effects. Spatial heterogeneity in pandemic-related shocks generated a temporary negative correlation between minimum wage increases and employment. We propose a procedure to control for pandemic shocks that eliminates this correlation. Our results hold among all jobs, restaurant workers, teens and in low-wage counties.

    Is New Platform Work Different than Other Freelancing?

    (with A. Garin, E. Jackson and D. Koustas)
    AEA Papers and Proceedings (2020)
    View Abstract | Paper | Appendix | Replication Materials

    The rise of freelance work in the online platform economy (OPE) has received considerable media and policy attention in recent years, but freelance work is by no means a new phenomenon. In this paper, we draw on I.R.S. tax records to identify instances when workers begin doing online platform work versus other freelance/independent contractor “gig” work for firms. We find gig work occurs around major reductions in outside income, and document usage over the lifecycle. Our results provide suggestive evidence on motivations for entering into each type of work.
